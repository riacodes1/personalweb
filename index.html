Research

Developed proficiencies in harnessing neural-network and machine-learning technologies for real-life applications through four research projects, including aiding in search-and-rescue missions, improving distance-learning during COVID-19, and classifying and correlating genes which can cause various cancers. I am also very grateful to my mentors and professors who have helped me along the way.

Search & Rescue

Research linked here.

  • The project aim was to simulate real-time, human-robot collaboration and test autonomous bots in a laboratory setting for search-and-rescue situations.

  • Using a neural network model and three separate types of autonomous bots (Zooids), the simulation was a continuous, goal-based interaction representing (a) the human in distress or the goal (b) the robot representing the first responder trying to meet the human in distress and (c) the AI robot leading the first-responder robot towards its goal.

  • Worked under Professor Dorsa Sadigh's ILIAD Lab in the Stanford Computer Science Department.

Virtual Reality for COVID-19

Research linked here.

  • Assembled multi-sensory dataset to produce sounds dynamically reflecting ways we interact with objects and paired this dataset with neural-network model trained on optical flow to detect nuanced hand movements

  • Applied artificial intelligence algorithms and techniques on a data set of ASMR videos to: a) Detect Hands in Videos using MediaPipe b) Extract Coordinates of Hand Landmarks c) Identify Occluded Hand Frames d) Investigate Several Optical Flow Techniques for Hand-Tracking e) Run Mask RCNN on an Annotated ASMR Dataset.

  • Working in the IPRL lab under Professor Jeannette Bohg.

Classifying Cancer

Research linked here.

  • Used several machine learning methods (k nearest neighbors, random hyperplane projection, decision trees, k means) to develop AI-powered system trained on thousands of genomic data to correlate specific genes with lung and prostate cancer.

  • Achieved 96% predicion accuracy using k nearest neighbors technique. Applications in improving gene-based therapies for these types of cancers using technology such as CRISPR. Presented research to leading PhDs and researchers at regional AI Banquet.

  • Selected to do this research as part of AI4ALL program.

Kuri Robot for Elderly Rehabilitation

  • After recognizing increasing need for elderly care and rehabilitation through empathetic, technological means, coded and developed artificial intelligence algorithms to enhance facial and visual recognition on entirely novel Kuri robot interface.

  • Self-learned Robot Operating System (ROS) and C++, and developed proficiencies in managing remote and local server interfaces. Enabling remote access on the robot, the system was ultimately successfully tested.

  • Invited back to lab after completing AI4ALL program to complete this research.

Published Books

To challenge the "Disney-diversity" stereotypes of people of color in both technology, wrote and published two novels in my series The Code Detectives, crafting powerful young female role models that break the "coders are nerds" stereotype and explain complex AI topics simply.

#1: The Code Con Crisis

Ramona Diaz and her best friend Lily are your typical middle school girls: they love having sleepovers, reading exciting books, and coding. But when the code for their climate change app Ocean goes missing two days before the Riverside Code Con, Ramona and Lily must put their coding abilities to the test using artificial intelligence and track down the thief. Is their project done for? Or will they be able to crack the code?

Concepts Introduced: neural networks, intro to artificial intelligence used for social good.

#2: The Student Council Sabotage

Ramona Diaz and her best friend Lily are excited: it's student council election season at Riverside School and their friend Tara is running for council chair! But when someone sabotages the campaign materials and posters, elections are in danger of being canceled. Will Ramona and Lily be able to use their coding expertise to save the day? Or will the student council saboteur come out on top?

Concepts Introduced: GPS technology, machine-learning, datasets, biases in datasets & AI algorithms.

Community

I believe that to truly push the boundaries of artificial intelligence technology to benefit humanity, accessible computer science education is a must. This means eliminating existing educational inequities that have resulted through the pervasive digital divide. Through my nonprofit and speaking engagements, I hope to spread this vision of equitable technology distribution and education.

CodeBuddies Nonprofit

  • Founded CodeBuddies with the goal of increasing access to technology education combined with interdisciplinary study to students in the local community.

  • Since then, CodeBuddies has hosted global technology challenges, workshops, and outreach events that have ultimately reached over 12,000+ individuals across 40+ countries

  • In response to heightened educational inequities posed by COVID-19, created free courses in technology & AI education that have been used by 500+ teachers and students.

Speaking Engagements

  • Advocated for public policy responses on AI ethics & diversity and tech education inequities across the nation as an invited speaker to several conferences, including the REWORK Deep Learning Summit in San Francisco.

  • In 2019, invited to speak to members of the U.S. House of Representatives about the importance of artificial intelligence education and need for equitable technology distribution to combat the pervasive digital divide.

  • In addition, spoke at several local hackathons and technology events, increasing enthusiasm and interest in technological empowerment.
